Why were <b> (bold) and <i> (italics) tags replaced by <strong> (strong) and <em> (emphasis) tags as the new standard in HTML 5?
The new tags separate content and style. The bold tag says to a browser to change the font to boldface essentially changing the content. The strong tag says to apply a strong emphasis to the text thereby applying a style. There's a second argument that the new tags provide extra meaning, "semantics", to your document.
